Chapter I — Before the Ping

Apocrypha (Probably)

In the first days of the internet-that-mattered, there were productivity apps, crypto threads, and six trillion unread emails. Servers stood empty, voice channels echoed with nobody, and no one had yet uttered the phrase, ‚ÄúWhy does the pig look like that?‚Äù

Chapter II — The First Image

Canon (lol)

One bored entity, name redacted by history and Discord TOS, opened MS Paint. They drew a pig. They gave it an expression scientists still cannot classify. They exported it as a suspiciously compressed JPEG and posted it into a tiny, forgotten channel with the caption: “he is wigger pig”.

Chapter III — The Oink Heard Across the Server

Confirmed by Screenshots

Notifications began to appear. One ping. Two. A flood. People joined just to say “what is that”, “delete this”, or “i am saving this forever”. The pig was reposted, cropped, color-inverted, and quoted out of context. The first reaction stack formed: üòÇüíÄüê∑üî•.

Chapter IV — Birth of the Cult

Community-Verified

As hours turned into whatever time is in Discord, users began assigning titles: Gym Pig, Drip Pig, Cult Pig, PresiPig. Roles were forged, emojis were minted, and someone said, “this is basically a religion now”. No one disagreed. The first unofficial doctrine was typed entirely in caps lock.

Chapter V — Dawn of the Wars

Foreshadowing, Unfortunately

With every new pig variant came arguments: Which pig is canon? Who owns the original file? Should the pig be allowed admin perms? Factions formed. Alliances broke. Someone made a tier list. Thus the foundations of the Pig Wars were quietly installed, like doomed DLC.

Relic: “wigger-pig-ranking-v1.png” — the tier list that started at least three lifelong grudges.

From Void to Pig — Dawn Timeline

A compressed, regret-filled sprint from empty internet to fully weaponized pig meme.

Historians of the AbyssVC council divide the prehistory of the server into two eras: BP (Before Pig) and AP (After Pig). BP was quiet. AP was notifications, late-night game shows, and people asking, “how is this still online?”.

What follows is the officially unofficial dawn timeline, reconstructed from screenshots, corrupted exports, and one mod who swears they remember everything despite never sleeping.
